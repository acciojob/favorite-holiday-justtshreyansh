Vrindavan


Vrindavan is a sacred town located in the Mathura district of Uttar Pradesh, India. It is one of the most revered pilgrimage sites for Hindus, especially devotees of Lord Krishna. According to Hindu mythology, Vrindavan is the place where Krishna spent his childhood and performed many of his divine pastimes (leelas), including the Rasa Lila with Radha and the gopis (cowherd girls)
